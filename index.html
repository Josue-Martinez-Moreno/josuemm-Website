Biography

Research & Others

Current Research

In mid-2017, I started my PhD in Earth Sciences at the Research School of Earth Sciences at the Australian National University under the advice of Prof. Andrew Hogg, Dr. Adele Morrison and Dr. Andrew Kiss. Currently, my research interest is focused on oceanic energy. In particular, my project has the goal to decompose the kinetic energy (KE) based on oceanic processes (i.e. eddies, jets, etc). Until now, we almost have separated the eddy’s contribution to KE using the algorithm “Trackeddy”. It uses a spatial tracking and geometrical analysis to extract eddies and their velocities. Preliminary results show that this decomposition could provide the impact, contribution and variation of each transient process to climate change.

Previous Research

In early 2017, I finished my bachelor degree with first class honours in Earth Sciences with a major in Aquatic Science. During the last year of my degree, I worked on my thesis using the MITgcm model under the advice of Prof. Angel Ruiz. We developed an experiment in the Gulf of Mexico, where passive particles were implemented. This research conducted us to understand and estimate the dispersion of “Pollutants” due to the Coatzacoalcos and Grijalva rivers plumes (in preparation).
